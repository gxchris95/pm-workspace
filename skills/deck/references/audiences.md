# Audience Profiles

Pick the profile that matches the brief. Each profile dictates length, depth, vocabulary, what to include, and what to leave out.

---

## Executive (CEO, CPO, CTO, GM)

- **Time budget**: 5–10 slides, 10–20 minutes max.
- **Mindset**: "What's the call, what's the risk, what do you need from me?"
- **Lead with**: the recommendation and the decision asked for.
- **Include**: 1-line situation, 1-line complication, the recommendation, top 3 risks with mitigations, the ask, key metrics with source. One chart max if it's the crux.
- **Skip**: feature-level detail, full roadmap timelines, internal team names, jargon, history.
- **Tone**: confident, declarative, numerical. No hedging adjectives ("really", "very", "quite").
- **Glance test**: 3 seconds per slide is mandatory.
- **Common ask**: approve / reject / fund / unblock / decide.

---

## Board

- **Time budget**: 8–15 slides, 30 minutes including Q&A.
- **Mindset**: "Is the strategy on track? What changed? What are the risks to the business?"
- **Lead with**: the headline metric and the strategic question of the quarter.
- **Include**: financial/usage metrics vs. plan, top 3 strategic moves and their status, top 3 risks with mitigations, competitive context (1 slide), forward asks.
- **Skip**: implementation detail, individual feature PRDs, internal politics.
- **Tone**: formal, precise, outcome-focused. Always YoY and vs. plan.
- **Charts**: clean, labeled, sourced. No 3D, no animations.
- **Mandatory**: append last quarter's commitments and status (kept / slipped / dropped).

---

## Engineering team

- **Time budget**: 10–20 slides, 30–45 minutes interactive.
- **Mindset**: "What are we building, why, what are the constraints, what's flexible?"
- **Lead with**: the problem and the user evidence.
- **Include**: user pain (with quotes), proposed solution, scope (in/out), dependencies, timeline, open technical questions, success metrics.
- **Skip**: business pep talk, market sizing fluff, exec politics.
- **Tone**: collaborative, specific, willing to expose unknowns.
- **Always**: invite pushback on scope and feasibility. List explicit non-goals.
- **Bonus**: include an architecture diagram (Mermaid in Marp works fine).

---

## Design / UX

- **Time budget**: 8–15 slides, 30 minutes.
- **Mindset**: "Who is the user, what's the journey, what's the experience we're targeting?"
- **Lead with**: the user moment we're trying to fix or create.
- **Include**: user persona/segment, journey map or current state, target experience, design constraints, success criteria, references/inspiration.
- **Skip**: business KPIs unless they shape design constraints, technical implementation.
- **Tone**: empathetic, evidence-grounded (real quotes, real session clips).
- **Pictures > prose** is strongest here.

---

## Partner / Customer (external)

- **Time budget**: 5–10 slides, 20–30 minutes.
- **Mindset**: "What's in it for us, what's the commitment, what's the timeline?"
- **Lead with**: the value to them, not the value to us.
- **Include**: their problem in their language, our solution, joint outcome, integration/partnership shape, timeline, who does what, success metrics, contact.
- **Skip**: internal roadmap detail, internal team structure, anything not legally sharable.
- **Tone**: professional, confident, no internal jargon.
- **Mandatory**: legal/IP review before sending. Confirm with the PM.

---

## All-hands / company-wide

- **Time budget**: 10–25 slides, 20–40 minutes.
- **Mindset**: "What did we ship, what's next, why does it matter, where do I fit?"
- **Lead with**: the wins and what they enabled (customer outcomes, not feature ship dates).
- **Include**: wins (with numbers), losses + what we learned, what's next this quarter/half, how teams plug in, shoutouts.
- **Skip**: anything that requires deep context to understand.
- **Tone**: warm, energetic, specific. Real names and real outcomes.
- **Pictures > prose**: yes — show the product, show real users, show real teams.

---

## Mixed audience

When the audience is mixed (e.g., exec + eng), default to the **highest-stakes** audience profile (usually exec) and add an appendix targeted at the secondary audience. Tell the presenter explicitly: "exec-first; appendix has eng detail for follow-up Q&A."
